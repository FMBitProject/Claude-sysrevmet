Statistical Interpretation Guide
Egger's Regression Test (Egger et al., BMJ 1997)
Regresses standardised effect (ES/SE) on precision (1/SE). Intercept ≠ 0 indicates asymmetry.
● p < 0.10 → possible asymmetry   ● p ≥ 0.10 → no significant asymmetry
Begg & Mazumdar Rank Correlation (Begg & Mazumdar, Biometrics 1994)
Kendall's τ between standardised ES and variance. Less sensitive than Egger's.
● |τ| > 0.3, p < 0.10 → possible asymmetry
Trim-and-Fill (Duval & Tweedie, 2000)
Iteratively trims asymmetric studies and imputes mirror studies. Provides bias-adjusted pooled estimate.
● R method used. If k₀ = 0: no asymmetry detected
Funnel Plot Asymmetry ≠ Publication Bias alone
Asymmetry may also result from: heterogeneity, selection bias, artefact (small-study effects), chance. Interpret in context. Use fail-safe N (Rosenthal) for supplementary evidence.

📈 Cumulative Meta-Analysis
Cumulative MA pools studies sequentially in chronological order, showing how the evidence evolved over time. Useful for identifying when evidence became stable and detecting temporal bias. Sort by: publication year (recommended), or study size.
τ² Estimator Comparison (DL vs REML vs PM)
Compares pooled estimates across all three τ² estimators. REML is considered the least biased for most scenarios (Viechtbauer 2005; Cochrane §10.4.2). Large differences suggest heterogeneity estimates are sensitive to method choice — report this as a sensitivity finding.

Network Meta-Analysis (NMA)
Compare ≥3 interventions simultaneously using direct and indirect evidence. Generates league tables, network plots, and SUCRA rankings. Based on frequentist graph-theoretic NMA (Rücker 2012; Chaimani 2013).
Network Setup — Interventions & Comparisons
NMA requires: Each study compares exactly 2 interventions. All interventions must form a connected network. Minimum 3 interventions. Effect measure must be consistent (all OR, or all MD, etc.).
Each line = one node in the network. First line = reference/comparator.
All ES as ratios (OR/RR/HR) — will be log-transformed internally.
Network Plot
Node size ∝ number of studies. Edge thickness ∝ number of direct comparisons.
Network Statistics
League Table — All Pairwise Comparisons
Read row vs column: upper triangle = NMA estimate (direct+indirect); lower triangle = direct evidence only (where available). Reference = first intervention defined above.
SUCRA Rankings (Surface Under the Cumulative Ranking Curve)
SUCRA (0–100%): higher = more likely to be the best intervention. SUCRA 100% = certainly best; 0% = certainly worst. Based on P-scores (frequentist equivalent, Rücker & Schwarzer 2015).
Inconsistency Assessment
Inconsistency = disagreement between direct and indirect evidence. Assessed via node-splitting and the I² of inconsistency. If present, the transitivity assumption may be violated.

Step-by-Step Workflow
Priority: Required — Must be done for journal submission Recommended — Strongly advised for Q1–Q2 Optional — Adds value for specific review types
1
Register on PROSPERO Required Before you start
Register your protocol at prospero.york.ac.uk before searching or extracting any data. Enter the CRD number in the PROSPERO field at the top of the screen. Required by BMJ, Lancet, Systematic Reviews journal, and most Q1 journals. Reviewers will check this during peer review.
2
Define PICO or PECO Framework Required Tab 1 — PICO
Fill in Population, Intervention or Exposure, Comparator, and Outcome. Complete all eligibility criteria fields below the PICO form and click Save Criteria to lock them before searching. This satisfies PRISMA 2020 Item 6 — eligibility criteria must be defined a priori (before searching), not after seeing the results.
3
Run Multi-Database Search Required Tab 1 — Search
Search at least two major databases (PubMed and one other). PRISMA 2020 requires you to report which databases were searched and the exact date. The search query is built automatically from your PICO fields. The search and all settings are saved to your Dashboard history so you can restore the session later.
4
Import RIS Files from Other Databases Recommended Tab 1 — RIS Import
Export records from Scopus, Web of Science, Covidence, or Mendeley as RIS files and import them here. Cochrane recommends searching at least three independent databases. Smart deduplication runs automatically using DOI matching and title comparison, so duplicates across sources are removed.
5
Screen Titles and Abstracts Required Tab 1 + Tab 10
Two reviewers must independently screen all titles and abstracts against the eligibility criteria. Click the Plus Extract button on studies that meet inclusion criteria. After screening, go to Tab 10 and enter the agreement matrix to calculate Cohen kappa. Q1 journals require kappa to be reported and most expect a value of 0.80 or above.
6
Update PRISMA Flow Diagram Required Tab 2
Fill in the PRISMA numeric inputs: records screened, records excluded at screening, full texts assessed, and full texts excluded. Add each exclusion reason with its count. The PRISMA 2020 flow diagram is generated automatically and can be included in your manuscript as a figure.
7
Extract Study Data Required Tab 3
For each included study, set the study design and choose the outcome type. Use Continuous for mean and SD, Dichotomous for events and total, or Pre-entered ES for adjusted HR and OR from observational papers. Use the AI Extractor to paste text from the paper and auto-fill fields. Data extraction must be done by two independent reviewers.
8
Assess Risk of Bias Required Tab 3 — Risk of Bias
Select the appropriate tool: RoB 2 for randomized trials, ROBINS-I for observational studies, NOS for cohort and case-control, or Mixed for reviews with both types. Assess all domains per study. Both reviewers must assess Risk of Bias independently. Record agreement in Tab 10.
9
Run Statistical Analysis Required Tab 4
Choose your statistical model. REML is recommended by Cochrane for most scenarios. Select the effect measure that matches your data type. Enable HKSJ correction, especially when the number of studies is below 20. Statistics update automatically as you enter data. The prediction interval is computed when three or more studies have valid data.
10
Generate and Download Forest Plot Required Tab 5
Open Tab 5 to render the SVG forest plot. Click Download SVG to save it as a vector file. Use this as Figure 1 in your manuscript. The plot shows each study effect estimate with confidence interval, the pooled diamond, and the prediction interval bar. RCT studies appear as squares and observational studies as diamonds.
11
Assess Publication Bias Required Tab 6
PRISMA 2020 Item 15 and 21 require reporting on risk of publication bias. Open Tab 6 to view the funnel plot and run Egger test, Begg test, and Trim-and-Fill. Egger test requires at least 10 studies for reliable results. Report all test results in the Methods and Results sections of your manuscript.
12
Run Subgroup Analysis Recommended Tab 7
Pre-specified subgroup analysis by study design (RCT vs observational), Risk of Bias level, or outcome type helps explain sources of heterogeneity. Run only subgroups that were pre-specified in your PROSPERO protocol. Any post-hoc subgroups must be clearly labeled as exploratory in your manuscript. Subgroup analysis requires at least two studies per subgroup.
13
Grade the Certainty of Evidence Recommended Tab 8
GRADE is required by most Q1 journals and all Cochrane reviews. Add each outcome, select the evidence type, and apply downgrade and upgrade domains. The Summary of Findings table is generated automatically and exported as a formatted HTML table ready for inclusion as Table 1 or a supplementary table.
14
Run Sensitivity Analyses Required Tab 9
At minimum, run the leave-one-out analysis and the high-Risk-of-Bias exclusion sensitivity. Also compare REML vs DL tau-squared estimates using the estimator comparison tool. If the results change substantially when high-risk-of-bias studies are removed, this is an important limitation to report. Use the Active toggle in Tab 3 to exclude individual studies and see the impact immediately.
15
Record Inter-Rater Reliability (Kappa) Required Tab 10
Enter the 2x2 agreement matrix for title and abstract screening, and separately for Risk of Bias assessment. Cohen kappa is calculated automatically. Report both kappa values in your Methods section. Most Q1 journals require kappa of at least 0.70 and consider 0.80 or above as excellent. Log all discordances and how they were resolved in the Discordance Log.
16
Run Meta-Regression Optional Tab 7
Use if you have at least 10 studies and a continuous covariate (publication year, sample size) that you hypothesize explains heterogeneity. Meta-regression with fewer than 10 studies is unreliable and should not be reported as a main finding. Always label meta-regression results as exploratory and hypothesis-generating.
17
Run Network Meta-Analysis Optional Tab 13
Use only when your review includes three or more competing interventions and you want to compare them simultaneously using both direct and indirect evidence. Requires a connected network where all interventions can be linked through comparisons. Check the inconsistency test results to verify the transitivity assumption is not violated.
18
Generate AI Paper Draft Optional Tab 12
After completing all required steps above, open Tab 12 to generate an AI-written draft manuscript. Enter metadata in Tab 11 first. The AI uses your actual statistics, study list, and GRADE results. Always review and verify the output before submitting. The AI draft is approximately 80 to 85 percent ready and requires your editing for final submission quality.
19
Export All Outputs and Submit Required Tab 11
Download all required submission files from Tab 11: full manuscript HTML (convert to Word or PDF for submission), PRISMA 2020 checklist (attach as required document), forest plot SVG (Figure 1), Risk of Bias traffic light table (Figure 2), GRADE Summary of Findings table, and the statistical supplement. Save your project as JSON for your records.

HKSJ Correction
The Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman correction uses a t-distribution with degrees of freedom equal to the number of studies minus one, instead of the standard normal distribution. This gives wider and more honest confidence intervals, especially important when the number of studies is below 20. Cochrane Handbook Section 10.4.4 recommends enabling HKSJ by default.
95 Percent Prediction Interval
The prediction interval shows the range where 95 percent of true effects in the underlying population are expected to lie. If the prediction interval includes the null value (OR equals 1, MD equals 0), the intervention may not be beneficial in all settings. Always report the prediction interval alongside the pooled estimate. Reference: Riley et al. BMJ 2011. Cochrane Section 10.7.5.

GRADE Certainty of Evidence
Reference: Guyatt GH et al. GRADE guidelines. Journal of Clinical Epidemiology. 2011. GRADE Working Group, grading.com.
Starting Certainty by Study Type
Randomized Trials start at HIGH
Randomized evidence starts at High certainty (four filled squares). It can only be downgraded by the five downgrade domains. It cannot be upgraded.
Observational Studies start at LOW
Observational evidence starts at Low certainty (two filled squares). It can be further downgraded or upgraded by up to three upgrade domains.
Five Downgrade Domains
DomainWhen to DowngradeKey Question
Risk of BiasMost included studies have serious or critical risk of bias across key domainsCould the result be explained by methodological flaws in the included studies?
InconsistencyI-squared above 50 percent, large tau-squared, or subgroup results point in different directionsAre the results consistent and pointing in the same direction across all included studies?
IndirectnessThe population, intervention, comparator, or outcome differs meaningfully from your review questionDoes the available evidence directly address the clinical question you are asking?
ImprecisionWide 95 percent confidence interval, crosses the null value, or total sample size is smallIs the effect estimate precise enough to make a clinical recommendation?
Publication BiasEgger test p below 0.10, visible funnel asymmetry, or selective outcome reporting suspectedCould missing or unpublished studies change the conclusion of this meta-analysis?
Three Upgrade Domains — Observational Evidence Only
DomainCriteria for Upgrading
Large EffectOR or RR greater than 2 or less than 0.5: upgrade by 1. OR or RR greater than 5 or less than 0.2: upgrade by 2. No major confounders.
Dose-Response GradientA clear biological gradient exists across levels of exposure or dose. Upgrade by 1.
Plausible Confounding Reduces EffectAll plausible residual confounders would reduce (not increase) the observed effect. Upgrade by 1.
Certainty Levels and Their Meaning
High — four squares We are very confident that the true effect is close to the estimated effect. The evidence comes from well-conducted randomized trials with consistent, precise results.
Moderate — three squares We are moderately confident in the estimated effect. The true effect is likely close to the estimate, but there is a possibility that it is substantially different.
Low — two squares Our confidence in the estimated effect is limited. The true effect may be substantially different from the estimate in this review.
Very Low — one square We have very little confidence in the estimated effect. The true effect is likely substantially different from the estimate in this review.

Frequently Asked Questions
► Do I need to install anything?
No. This is a single HTML file that runs entirely in your browser. No internet is required for statistics, forest plot, funnel plot, GRADE, or exports. Internet is only required for database search and AI features.
► Can I use this for observational studies only?
Yes. Select Observational Only as the review type in Tab 1. Use ROBINS-I or NOS for Risk of Bias assessment. Choose REML as the statistical model. Enter HR or OR values using the Pre-entered ES option in Tab 3. GRADE starts at Low certainty for observational evidence.
► My search returned 0 results. Why?
Try broadening your search terms. Remove the Comparator field from PICO to widen the query. Check that you have an active internet connection. Try disabling strict study design filters. Make sure at least one database checkbox is checked. Try searching PubMed only first to verify connectivity.
► How do I enter an adjusted HR from a paper that only reports the adjusted value?
In Tab 3, find the study row and set Outcome Type to Pre-entered ES. Enter the HR value in the Effect field. Enter the lower and upper bounds of the 95 percent confidence interval. The standard error is calculated automatically from the CI. In Tab 4, set the Effect Measure to HR.
► What is the difference between I-squared and tau-squared?
I-squared is the percentage of total variability in effect estimates that is due to between-study heterogeneity rather than chance. Tau-squared is the absolute estimated variance of true effects across studies. Both should be reported. A low I-squared combined with a large tau-squared can still indicate substantial heterogeneity when individual study variances are also small.
► Should I use DL, REML, or PM?
REML is recommended by Cochrane Handbook Section 10.4.2 because it produces less biased tau-squared estimates compared to DL. Use the tau-squared estimator comparison in Tab 9 to see how much the pooled results differ across the three methods. If the results differ substantially, report all three and discuss this as a sensitivity finding in your manuscript.
► My forest plot is empty after entering data. What is wrong?
Check that the Effect Measure selected in Tab 4 matches the data type you entered in Tab 3. Continuous data requires MD or SMD. Dichotomous data requires OR, RR, or RD. Pre-entered data requires HR or IRR. Also make sure all required fields are filled: for continuous data you need N, mean, and SD for both groups. For dichotomous data you need events and total for both groups.
► Can I use this tool offline?
Yes. All statistical calculations, forest plot, funnel plot, GRADE assessment, sensitivity analyses, and all export functions work completely offline. Database search requires internet to connect to PubMed, Europe PMC, and other APIs. The AI Data Extractor and AI Paper Generator require internet to connect to the Claude API.
► How do I cite this tool in my paper?
In your Methods section, you can write: Analyses were conducted using Meta-Analysis Workspace Pro version 2. Statistical pooling used the DerSimonian-Laird or REML random-effects model with Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman correction. Standardized mean differences were calculated as Hedges g. Publication bias was assessed using Egger regression (Egger 1997), Begg rank correlation (Begg 1994), and the Trim-and-Fill method (Duval and Tweedie 2000). Certainty of evidence was graded using the GRADE approach (Guyatt 2011).
► The AI paper generator produces generic text. How do I improve it?
Fill in as much data as possible before generating. Make sure PICO fields are completed in Tab 1 and eligibility criteria are saved. Enter all study data in Tab 3 and run statistics in Tab 4. Complete the GRADE assessment in Tab 8. Add an Additional Context description in Tab 12, for example write the target audience is cardiologists or focus on the clinical implications for primary care. The more complete your data, the more specific and useful the generated text will be.
► How do I run a Network Meta-Analysis?
Open Tab 13 and click Load Example Data to see the required format. Define all your interventions one per line in the first text box. The first intervention listed becomes the reference comparator. Then enter each comparison on a separate line in the format: Study name, vertical bar, Intervention A vs Intervention B, vertical bar, effect size, vertical bar, CI lower, vertical bar, CI upper. All interventions must form a connected network for the analysis to run.
